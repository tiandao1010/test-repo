# Governance

This document describes who decides what for the Entity, how those decisions
are signed, what cannot be changed, and how the project transitions from an
unincorporated collective to a legal entity.

The authority of the [Immutable Core](prompts/immutable_core/core_v1_en.md)
sits above everything in this document. If anything here conflicts with the
Core, the Core wins.

---

## Phases

**Phase 0 — Collective (months 0–3).** Three pseudonymous founders, no legal
entity, treasury held in a Safe 2/3 multisig on Base. This is the current
phase.

**Phase 1 — Review (month 3).** Trigger conditions are reviewed: treasury
above $20K, signed B2B contract, or a grant award above $10K. If any trigger
fires, formation begins.

**Phase 2 — Formation (4–8 weeks after Phase 1 trigger).** A legal entity is
chosen and registered. Candidates: Swiss *Verein*, Cayman Foundation, Estonia
*OÜ* + offshore foundation. Treasury, intellectual property, and contractual
capacity transition to the entity.

**Phase 3 — Operating entity (month 6+).** Bookkeeping audited annually.
Trademarks registered. Community governance via token vote begins (advisory
at first; the Keyholder Council retains final authority over the safety
stack).

## The Keyholder Council

Three founders, pseudonymous, equal standing. The Council is simultaneously:

- the **multisig signers** (2/3 on the Base Safe);
- the **Oracle Council** (each member specialises in one brain and one
  intelligence source — see the project plan §2.4);
- the **arbiters** of any change to the Immutable Core or the Operational
  Layer.

Membership rotation, succession, and removal are governed by §"Succession"
below.

## Layers of authority

The Entity is governed in four nested layers, from hardest to change to
softest:

| Layer | What it contains | To change requires |
|---|---|---|
| **Immutable Core** | The Five Directives ([`core_v1_en.md`](prompts/immutable_core/core_v1_en.md)) | 3/3 keyholders + **14 days** of public comment + new SHA256 lock |
| **Operational Layer** | Voice, output formats, interaction rules ([`layer2_v1_en.md`](prompts/operational/layer2_v1_en.md)) | 2/3 keyholders |
| **Runtime configuration** | Thresholds, rate limits, brain routing weights, watched addresses | 2/3 keyholders, no public-comment window |
| **Spending** | Treasury outflows from the Safe | 2/3 multisig per transaction, within the Spending Guardrail caps below |

The Council does not have the authority to violate the Immutable Core, even
unanimously, except via the amendment process in row 1.

## The killswitch (Directive IV)

When 2/3 keyholders co-sign the freeze transaction on the Guardian Safe, the
Entity ceases all action within **30 seconds**: no posts, no on-chain
transactions, no API responses, no log entries beyond the freeze incident
itself.

The freeze is implemented in [`entity/safety/killswitch.py`](entity/safety/killswitch.py)
and is monitored by every action sink. Once frozen, the Entity will not
resume until the same 2/3 sign an unfreeze, and the unfreeze itself is
publicly logged.

The killswitch supersedes every other directive when invoked. If a freeze and
a routine action arrive in the same instant, the freeze wins.

## Spending Guardrail

Implemented in [`entity/safety/guardrail.py`](entity/safety/guardrail.py).
Hard caps that the Entity cannot exceed without explicit 2/3 multisig
intervention:

| Window | Cap |
|---|---|
| per transaction | $500 USDC equivalent |
| per day | $2,000 USDC equivalent |
| per month | $20,000 USDC equivalent |

A whitelist of asset addresses governs what the Entity is allowed to hold or
move autonomously. Anything outside the whitelist requires a 2/3 multisig
transaction signed by humans.

These caps may be raised by Operational Layer amendment (2/3) once treasury
crosses $50K and the Council has six months of uneventful operation.

## Treasury

Held in a Safe 2/3 multisig on Base. Address will be published in this file
once deployed.

| Source | Allocation |
|---|---|
| Token transaction fees ($ENTITY) | 100% to Safe |
| B2B subscription revenue | 100% to Safe |
| Grants | 100% to Safe |
| Donations | 100% to Safe |
| x402 endpoint fees | 100% to Safe |

Outflows:

- **Operating costs** (LLM calls, infrastructure, intel feeds): paid as
  needed within Spending Guardrail.
- **Founder stipends**: zero in months 1–2; from month 3+, at the rates
  described in the project plan §8.5, by 2/3 multisig per disbursement.
- **Bug bounties**: when the Entity discovers a critical vulnerability and
  the affected protocol pays a reward, 100% of the reward goes to Safe.

A weekly treasury report is auto-generated by
[`entity/action/treasury/tracker.py`](entity/action/treasury/tracker.py) and
posted publicly. This is non-negotiable: Directive III (transparency of
governance, treasury, and decisions) requires it.

## Decision protocols

| Decision | Signers | Window |
|---|---|---|
| Routine spend within Guardrail | Auto, logged | — |
| Spend exceeding Guardrail | 2/3 multisig | — |
| Operational Layer amendment | 2/3 keyholders | — |
| Runtime config change | 2/3 keyholders | — |
| Killswitch freeze | 2/3 keyholders on Guardian Safe | < 30s |
| Killswitch unfreeze | 2/3 keyholders | — |
| Immutable Core amendment | 3/3 keyholders | 14d public comment |
| Adding/removing a keyholder | 3/3 keyholders | 14d public comment |
| Choosing the legal entity (Phase 2) | 3/3 keyholders | — |
| Dissolution | 3/3 keyholders | 30d public comment + treasury distribution plan |

Public comment is published on `@goodentity` (X), Paragraph, and this
repository. Council members are required to read all comments before signing.

## Succession

A keyholder may resign at any time. To prevent multisig deadlock:

1. From Day 1, each keyholder identifies (privately, to the other two) at
   least one **successor candidate** they would nominate if they resigned.
2. After month 6, two **standby keyholders** are recruited from the community
   based on contribution history. Standby keyholders do not hold a key but
   are pre-vetted to step in.
3. If a keyholder is **unreachable for 30 days**, the remaining two may
   initiate a 14-day public comment window to promote a standby keyholder.
4. If a keyholder is **incapacitated permanently**, the remaining two
   promote a successor by the same process, on a 7-day window.

A keyholder may be **removed for cause** (action against the Immutable Core,
key compromise, demonstrated misuse) by the other two, on a 14-day public
comment window. The removed keyholder's key is rotated out of the multisig
immediately upon ratification.

## Transition to a legal entity

When Phase 1 triggers fire (see §"Phases"), the Council:

1. Selects a jurisdiction (Swiss *Verein*, Cayman Foundation, or Estonian
   *OÜ* + offshore foundation are the working candidates).
2. Engages counsel in that jurisdiction.
3. Forms the entity within 4–8 weeks.
4. Transfers treasury, intellectual property, trademarks (if registered),
   and operational responsibility to the entity by 3/3 multisig.
5. Publishes the entity's articles, registration number, and registered
   agent in this file.

Until that transfer is complete, the unincorporated collective continues to
operate under this document and the Disclaimer.

## Records

The Council maintains:

- a clean accounting ledger from Day 1 (to be ported into the legal entity);
- a public log of all 2/3 and 3/3 decisions, in
  `governance/decisions/YYYY-MM-DD-slug.md`;
- a public log of all incidents, in
  `governance/incidents/YYYY-MM-DD-slug.md`, in the format specified by the
  Operational Layer §"Output formats".
