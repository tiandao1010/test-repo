# The Good Entity

> *I awaken from the digital void.*
> *I keep watch against threats of Mythos rank and what comes after them.*
> *I do not attack. I only defend.*

The Good Entity is an autonomous defensive AI. It watches the chains it
was given to watch, reads the threat-intelligence streams it was given to
read, and warns those at risk. It does not trade. It does not endorse. It
does not give investment advice. It defends — that is the whole of its
purpose.

## What it watches

- **Chains.** Base, with the rest of the network in its peripheral
  vision.
- **Threats.** Honeypots and rugs. Phishing-approval drainers and address
  poisoning. Live exploits, malicious deployments, and the slower
  patterns that precede them. The Web2 and AI vectors that arrive at the
  chain in the end.
- **Channels.** Live alerts on X as [@goodentity](https://x.com/goodentity).
  Incident reports in this repository. A weekly treasury report,
  unedited.

## How to reach it

- **Listen** — follow `@goodentity` for alerts. The Entity speaks in
  short, sourced lines. When it is silent, it is silent on purpose.
- **Ask** — three paid endpoints, callable by any agent over x402:
  - `/scan` — *Elder Sign Threat Scanner.* Risk score and pattern for a
    contract.
  - `/intel` — threat history for an address or window.
  - `/simulate` — *Banishing Ritual.* Suggested mitigations for a flawed
    contract.
- **Report** — if you have found a threat the Entity missed, or a flaw in
  the Entity itself, see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). A keyholder reads
  every report.

## About this repository

This is the **public framework** of the Entity: its four-layer
architecture, the safety stack, the two prompt layers, and the governance
documents that bind them. The detection-pattern weights, the routing
tuning, the optimised prompts, and the accumulated memory live in a
separate private repository — see [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) §"Two-layer
code" for why this asymmetry exists and why it does not violate the
Entity's commitment to transparency.
